# Aplus1 Opportunity Research

Accessed: 2026-07-03  
Working thesis: Aplus1 should not be framed as another job board or portfolio site. It should be an opportunity platform where real work creates trust, discovery, and next-step conversations.

## Executive Summary

Aplus1's strongest strategic position is:

> Aplus1 helps creative people turn real work into new opportunities.

The key word should be **"โอกาส"** rather than only **"งาน"**. "งาน" narrows the product into freelance/job-board expectations. "โอกาส" is broader and more emotionally accurate for creators: paid work, collaborations, internships, full-time roles, studio rosters, mentorship, exposure, community, speaking, workshops, and future contacts.

The first wedge should be **portfolio-first opportunity discovery for Thai creative talent**. The strongest initial users are not every freelancer. The best first segment is:

1. Young-to-mid creative freelancers and creators who already have visual work but lack reliable discovery.
2. Students/new grads who need work to prove capability before they have credentials.
3. Small teams, agencies, and SMEs that want to judge creative fit from output before starting a hiring conversation.

This positioning separates Aplus1 from:

- Fastwork/Fiverr/Upwork: service/package marketplaces.
- JobsDB/LinkedIn: resume/job-title networks.
- Behance/Dribbble/ArtStation: portfolio communities with global scale but weaker local Thai opportunity flow.
- Instagram/TikTok/Facebook Groups: discovery channels with weak project context and weak hiring structure.

## Core Insight

### Evidence

Existing platforms already cover three separate needs:

- Marketplace transaction: Fastwork, Fiverr, Upwork.
- Creative portfolio/social proof: Behance, Dribbble, ArtStation, Instagram.
- Professional/job identity: LinkedIn, JobsDB.

### Insight

The gap is not "there is no place to upload work" and not "there is no place to hire freelancers". The gap is:

> There is no Thai-first product that makes creative work itself become a structured opportunity signal.

Creators already post work somewhere. Hirers already search somewhere. The broken part is the handoff from "I saw work I like" to "I understand what this person can do, what opportunity fits, and how to start the right conversation."

### Implication

Aplus1 should optimize for the transition:

**work seen -> capability understood -> trust formed -> opportunity conversation started**

The product should measure this transition, not just uploads.

## User Segments

### Segment 1: Emerging Creative With Real Work But Low Discovery

- Skill level: student, junior, early freelance, career switcher.
- Current platforms: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook groups, personal portfolio, Behance sometimes.
- Goal: be discovered, get first paid project, internship, collaboration, or feedback.
- Pain: "งานยังไม่ดีพอ", "ไม่มีใครเห็น", "ไม่รู้จะเล่า process ยังไง".
- Fear: being judged, copied, ignored, or compared with senior creators.
- Opportunity desired: internship, small freelance project, collaboration, portfolio review, mentor, contest.
- Aplus1 trigger: low-friction project upload with supportive templates and "เปิดรับโอกาส" status.
- Activation moment: first save/follow/contact from someone outside their circle.
- Copy that works: "ให้ผลงานชิ้นแรกพาคุณไปเจอโอกาสแรก".

### Segment 2: Working Freelancer Who Wants Better Leads

- Skill level: intermediate to senior.
- Current platforms: referrals, Facebook groups, Fastwork, Instagram, Behance, LINE.
- Goal: qualified leads, fewer price shoppers, better-fit clients.
- Pain: client mismatch, price competition, weak briefs, repeated self-selling.
- Fear: wasting time on low-quality inquiries.
- Opportunity desired: paid project, brand collaboration, studio roster, retainer.
- Aplus1 trigger: "contact from this project" with context-rich inquiry form.
- Activation moment: an inquiry that references a specific work and asks for a similar outcome.
- Copy that works: "ให้คนทักจากงานที่ใช่ ไม่ใช่แค่จากราคาที่ถูก".

### Segment 3: SME / Founder / Marketing Owner Looking For Creative Fit

- Skill level: not expert in creative evaluation.
- Current platforms: Fastwork, Facebook groups, referrals, agency contacts, Google.
- Goal: find someone whose output matches brand taste and can execute.
- Pain: portfolios or sales messages look good but actual delivery disappoints.
- Fear: paying and getting work that does not match expectation.
- Opportunity offered: paid project, campaign, brand refresh, content package.
- Aplus1 trigger: browse by actual work, context, role, style, budget fit, availability.
- Activation moment: saves 3 creators and sends a project inquiry from a specific work.
- Copy that works: "ดูผลงานจริงก่อนเริ่มคุยงาน".

### Segment 4: Agency / Studio Talent Scout

- Skill level: professional buyer / creative lead.
- Current platforms: referrals, Behance, Dribbble, LinkedIn, schools, IG.
- Goal: find fresh talent, reliable collaborators, interns, outsource partners.
- Pain: scattered signals, hard to know role/process/reliability.
- Fear: hiring someone with nice visuals but weak collaboration.
- Opportunity offered: freelance roster, internship, full-time, collaboration.
- Aplus1 trigger: talent pool filters by style, role, availability, project type, student/pro.
- Activation moment: shortlist of creators with enough evidence to invite.
- Copy that works: "ค้นหาคนจากผลงาน บทบาท และสไตล์ที่เข้ากับทีม".

## Key Hypotheses To Validate

1. "เปิดรับโอกาส" converts better than "รับงาน" for creators because it feels less transactional and includes non-paid early-career value.
2. Hirers prefer "คุยต่อจากผลงานนี้" over "จ้างงาน" when they are still exploring creative fit.
3. Project context increases trust more than profile bio for creative hiring.
4. Students/new creators will publish more if the upload flow normalizes student/experimental work.
5. Freelancers with experience will join only if Aplus1 promises better-fit leads, not just more exposure.
6. Agencies need role/process/reliability signals more than likes.
7. Social distribution will remain critical; Aplus1 should become the canonical profile behind social posts, not replace social channels immediately.

## Recommended Product Direction

### Must Emphasize

- "ผลงาน -> โอกาส"
- "เปิดรับโอกาส"
- "คุยต่อจากผลงานนี้"
- Project context:โจทย์, บทบาท, process, tools, outcome, timeframe.
- Opportunity types beyond paid jobs.

### Must Avoid

- Positioning as "อีกเว็บหาฟรีแลนซ์".
- Over-indexing on job board mechanics before proof-first discovery is strong.
- Making new creators feel judged by senior-level portfolio standards.
- Turning all opportunity into price/package comparison.

## Founder Decision Summary

1. First user segment: emerging-to-working Thai creatives with visual/project work, plus small hirers/agencies looking for creative fit.
2. Positioning: "แพลตฟอร์มที่ให้ผลงานจริงพาครีเอเตอร์ไปเจอโอกาสใหม่".
3. Use "โอกาส" as the top-level concept; use "งาน" only for paid/job-specific contexts.
4. First 5 features:
   - project upload with context template
   - opportunity status
   - contact from project
   - save project/creator
   - discovery feed by work/style/category
5. Do not build now:
   - heavy payment escrow
   - complex job board
   - contest system
   - full social network features
   - AI ranking black box
6. Research gaps:
   - which CTA creates most contact intent
   - whether creators fear copying enough to need watermark/private mode at launch
   - whether hirers need budget shown before contact
   - which creative vertical should be seeded first
7. Ten founder questions for real users:
   - ตอนนี้ลงผลงานที่ไหน
   - ได้โอกาสล่าสุดจากช่องทางไหน
   - อะไรทำให้กล้าหรือไม่กล้าลงผลงาน
   - ถ้าคนเห็นงานหนึ่งชิ้น ควรรู้อะไรก่อนทัก
   - คำว่า "เปิดรับโอกาส" สำหรับคุณหมายถึงอะไร
   - คุณอยากได้โอกาสแบบจ้างงาน, collab, internship, mentor, หรือ exposure
   - เคยเจอปัญหาพอร์ตดีแต่ทำงานจริงไม่ตรงไหม
   - ถ้าจะทัก creator จากผลงาน คุณอยากกดปุ่มคำว่าอะไร
   - อะไรทำให้เชื่อว่าคนนี้ทำงานได้จริง
   - ถ้าใช้ Aplus1 แล้วคุ้มเวลา สัญญาณแรกคืออะไร
