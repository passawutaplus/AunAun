# Aplus1 Competitor Teardown

Accessed: 2026-07-03

## Strategic Frame

Aplus1 is not competing with one category. It sits between four markets:

1. Portfolio/community platforms.
2. Freelance marketplaces.
3. Job/professional networks.
4. Social discovery channels.

The strategic risk is being perceived as a weaker version of all four. The strategic opportunity is becoming the Thai-first product that combines:

**real creative work + opportunity status + context + next-step conversation**

## Competitor Evidence Notes

### Fastwork

Evidence:

- Official page positions as a Thai freelance marketplace and lists a very broad service taxonomy: AI, marketing, design, web/app, lifestyle, photo/video, legal, business advisory, writing, and more.
- Source: https://fastwork.co/

Insight:

- Fastwork is excellent when the buyer already knows the task and wants a provider.
- It is weaker when the buyer starts from taste, style, creative fit, or inspiration.

Aplus1 implication:

- Do not say "จ้างฟรีแลนซ์ง่ายขึ้น" as the primary promise.
- Say "ดูผลงานจริงก่อนเริ่มคุยโอกาส".

### Behance

Evidence:

- Behance has Explore, Jobs, Hire, and Share Work.
- Jobs page shows freelance and full-time creative jobs, salary ranges, remote tags, and categories.
- Hire page emphasizes AI-powered matches, conversations, file sharing, video calls, secure payment, and 50M+ community members.
- Sources:
  - https://www.behance.net/joblist
  - https://www.behance.net/hire

Insight:

- Behance has already evolved beyond portfolio hosting into opportunity infrastructure.
- But global scale creates noise. Thai creators may not feel visible, and Thai hirers may not feel operationally supported.

Aplus1 implication:

- Aplus1 needs local language, local hiring patterns, and easier "contact from this work" flow.

### Dribbble

Evidence:

- Dribbble hiring page says clients can work with designers on freelance, contract, part-time, or full-time basis.
- It offers freelancer hiring, job posting, payment protection, and hiring suite.
- Source: https://dribbble.com/hiring

Insight:

- Dribbble is highly design-forward, but "shot culture" can over-reward visual polish over process/outcome.

Aplus1 implication:

- Project pages should reward context and role, not just beautiful thumbnails.

### Contra

Evidence:

- Contra homepage presents independent professionals via projects, categories, earned amounts, messages, and visible project cards.
- Source: https://contra.com/

Insight:

- Contra is the closest strategic reference: project-first independent identity connected to work.

Aplus1 implication:

- This is a warning and a model. Aplus1 must not merely copy Contra; it must become a Thai opportunity graph around creative evidence.

### ArtStation

Evidence:

- Navigation includes Gallery, Blogs, Learning, Challenges, Marketplace, Job Listings, Hiring Studios, Post a Job, Find an Artist, Find a Studio.
- Source: https://www.artstation.com/jobs

Insight:

- Strong vertical platforms create loops: showcase -> learn -> challenge -> job -> hiring.

Aplus1 implication:

- Later growth can include briefs/challenges/learning, but MVP should first solve showcase -> contact.

### LinkedIn

Evidence:

- LinkedIn Jobs page says "Millions of jobs and people hiring" and promotes Open To Work, messaging, staying updated, finding people, and learning skills.
- Source: https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/

Insight:

- LinkedIn is the professional identity network. It is not optimized for creative proof.

Aplus1 implication:

- Aplus1 profiles should be linkable evidence from LinkedIn/social bios: "see real work and opportunity status here."

### JobsDB

Evidence:

- JobsDB Thailand is a job search/recruitment site with job search, profile search, salary, employment type, work arrangement, and classifications.
- Source: https://th.jobsdb.com/

Insight:

- JobsDB wins structured job search; Aplus1 should not be a general job board.

Aplus1 implication:

- If Aplus1 adds jobs, they should be opportunity cards tied to creative work and fit, not generic job listings.

## Positioning Map

| Axis | Left | Right | Aplus1 Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starting point | service/package | real work | strongly real work |
| Hiring mode | transaction-first | relationship/opportunity-first | opportunity-first with optional paid inquiry |
| Identity | resume/profile | portfolio/context | portfolio context |
| Discovery | keyword search | visual/style discovery | visual + context + opportunity status |
| Proof | reviews/ratings | work/process/outcome | work/process/outcome first, reviews later |
| Market | global scale | local relevance | Thai-first, global-ready later |

## White Space

The strongest white space:

> A local creative opportunity platform where every project can become a doorway to a paid, collaborative, learning, or career opportunity.

This is different from:

- "จ้างฟรีแลนซ์" because not every opportunity is a job.
- "ลงพอร์ต" because publishing alone is not enough.
- "โซเชียล" because Aplus1 adds context and intent.
- "หางาน" because it begins with proof, not resume.

## Product Lessons From Competitors

| Lesson | Seen In | Apply To Aplus1 |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring is moving into portfolio platforms. | Behance, Dribbble, Contra | Add opportunity CTAs early. |
| Trust needs more than visual appeal. | Behance testimonials/payment, Dribbble payment holding | Add project context, role, process, and eventually reviews. |
| Social reach matters. | Instagram/TikTok/Facebook | Make projects shareable and preview-rich. |
| Local habits matter. | Fastwork, JobsDB, LINE usage from DataReportal | Thai copy, LINE-friendly contact/share, local budget norms. |
| Vertical communities compound. | ArtStation | Seed one or two creative verticals before going broad. |

## Recommended Competitive Claim

Primary:

> Aplus1 คือพื้นที่ที่ให้ผลงานจริงพาครีเอเตอร์ไปเจอโอกาสใหม่

For hirers:

> ค้นหาคนจากผลงานจริง ก่อนเริ่มคุยโอกาส

For creators:

> ลงผลงานให้คนเห็นศักยภาพ ไม่ใช่แค่สมัครงาน

## Do Not Compete On

- Cheapest freelancer.
- Most jobs.
- Most followers.
- General resume database.
- Full payment escrow at MVP.
- Viral social feed at MVP.

## Compete On

- Thai creative context.
- Work-first discovery.
- Opportunity language.
- Project-specific contact.
- Trust through process and role.
- Better first opportunity for emerging creators.
